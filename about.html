The Story

Where curiosity
meets craft.

I'm Ritika Sharma — a nanoscience researcher and science communicator on a mission to make cutting-edge science accessible, beautiful, and impossible to ignore.

My journey started in a lab, staring at electron microscope images and wondering why the world didn't know how extraordinary this looked. That question led me to science art, then to science communication, then to building communities of researchers who believe that the way we present science is as important as the science itself.

Today I wear many hats — researcher, writer, speaker, visual artist — but they're all in service of the same goal: closing the gap between the lab bench and the people who should care about what happens there.

Research Focus

What I investigate

My research sits at the intersection of nanoscience, materials characterisation, and biophysics — probing matter at the scale where quantum effects start to matter and conventional physics gives way to new phenomena.

Nanostructure Synthesis
Designing and fabricating nanoscale materials with targeted optical and electronic properties.
Materials Characterisation
Advanced microscopy and spectroscopy — from electron imaging to single-molecule techniques.
Data Visualisation
Turning complex experimental datasets into clear, compelling visual narratives for research and public communication.
Science Communication
Building bridges between researchers and the public through writing, visual media, and educational outreach.
